Having had a spiritual awakening as a result of these steps, we tried to carry this message to alcoholics, and to practice these principles in all our affairs.

Practicing 12 Spiritual Principles in All Our Affairs

AA is a program of action. This page is a slow walk through each principle as a verb we can practice today in our homes, at work, in relationships, and in our own heads. Like a living tree, each action grows from the previous roots, branches into the next, and drops seeds that restart the loop.

Practice Loop Scroll slowly; notice how each action grows the next and returns us to the beginning.

01 - Honesty Step One

Honesty

Choosing to tell the whole truth, even when it's awkward.

Honesty is the root that makes every other principle possible. We admit what is actually happening in our head, at home, and at work. We stop hiding pieces of the story and ask someone safe to hear it. We practice telling the truth quickly and kindly in all our affairs.

Honesty - Step One practice
02 - Hope Step Two

Hope

Letting light in because truth broke the denial.

Hope grows when honesty cracks denial and lets in light; it points us toward faith. We imagine life not run by fear and keep showing up even when feelings lag. We notice small changes and name them out loud. We practice hope as a daily choice across all our affairs: family, work, and our inner talk.

Hope - Step Two practice
03 - Faith Step Three

Faith

Trusting enough to act, not just to believe.

Faith grows out of hope and leans into courage. We place our weight on a Power greater than ourselves and take the next right action. We match prayer with footwork because faith without works fades. In all our affairs we trust the guidance we ask for, one choice at a time.

Faith - Step Three practice
04 - Courage Step Four

Courage

Doing the next right hard thing with an open heart.

Courage grows from faith and leads into deeper integrity. We look at our patterns on paper, tell the whole truth, and keep showing up sober to life. We make the scary phone call, apologize in person, or sit still when running feels easier. Practicing courage in all our affairs keeps us moving toward integrity instead of hiding.

Courage - Step Four practice
05 - Integrity Step Five

Integrity

Bringing inside and outside into the same light.

Integrity flows from courage and feeds willingness. We say what we mean and do what we say at home, at work, and when no one is watching. We confess the full story to another human to clean out the corners. Each honest act plants willingness to let more be changed in all our affairs so insides and outsides can meet.

Integrity - Step Five practice
06 - Willingness Step Six

Willingness

Opening our hands so change can actually happen.

Willingness grows out of integrity and prepares us for humility. We let go, finger by finger, of the defects that block us, asking to be changed rather than muscling it. We practice being teachable in traffic, meetings, and conversations with family. This openness keeps the loop moving toward humility in all our affairs.

Willingness - Step Six practice
07 - Humility Step Seven

Humility

Standing at our true size and asking for help.

Humility grows from willingness and deepens love. We remember we are part of, not the center of, and we ask for strength we do not generate. We listen before we speak and accept correction without collapse. Practicing humility in all our affairs frees space for love to move through us.

Humility - Step Seven practice
08 - Love Step Eight

Love

Letting compassion lead the cleanup.

Love grows from humility and energizes discipline. We approach amends with gentleness and courage, repairing what we can without self-justifying. We choose patience with ourselves and others instead of punishment. Daily acts of care in all our affairs fuel the discipline to keep showing up.

Love - Step Eight practice
09 - Discipline Step Nine

Discipline

Showing up steady, not grand.

Discipline grows from love and sustains perseverance. We keep simple routines - prayer, inventory, meetings, service - whether or not we feel inspired. We make living amends through consistent behavior, not speeches. That steadiness trains us to persevere in all our affairs when life gets ordinary or hard.

Discipline - Step Nine practice
10 - Perseverance Step Ten

Perseverance

Staying with the work when the shine wears off.

Perseverance grows from discipline and sharpens awareness. We keep sweeping our side of the street and taking daily inventory instead of waiting for crisis. We recommit after setbacks without drama. Practicing perseverance in all our affairs keeps us awake enough to notice what needs attention.

Perseverance - Step Ten practice
11 - Awareness Step Eleven

Awareness

Listening on purpose for guidance.

Awareness grows from perseverance and clears the channel for service. We pause, pray, meditate, and watch our motives throughout the day. We notice fear and selfishness early and course-correct. By staying conscious in all our affairs, we become available to serve.

Awareness - Step Eleven practice
12 - Service Step Twelve

Service

Carrying the message by living it.

Service grows from all the previous work and carries the seed back to honesty and the newcomer. We share experience, strength, and hope while keeping the focus on action. We show up, sponsor, make coffee, and practice kindness at home and work alike. Giving it away helps us practice every principle again in all our affairs.

Service - Step Twelve practice
