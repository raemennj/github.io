Having had a spiritual awakening as a result of these steps, we tried to carry this message to alcoholics, and to practice these principles in all our affairs.

12 Spiritual Principles

A quiet, scrollable walk through the principles that shape your life. Each section holds a single idea, a single color, and a single invitation to act differently today.

Journey Scroll slowly. Breathe. Notice where you pause.
Scroll to reveal each principle
01 — Honesty Step One

Honesty

Telling the truth, especially to yourself.

Notice where you are pretending. The first movement of change is dropping the act—one small admission at a time.

Honesty · Step One
02 — Hope Step Two

Hope

Maybe I don’t have to live like this forever.

Hope is a tiny crack in the wall. Let in a bit of light. Let yourself imagine a life that’s not run by fear or compulsion.

Hope · Step Two
03 — Faith Step Three

Faith

Trusting something wiser than your fear.

You don’t have to know how it all works. Just move your weight a little off of yourself and onto something steadier.

Faith · Step Three
04 — Courage Step Four

Courage

Looking inward without turning away.

Courage isn’t loud. It’s steady. It lets you look at your life on paper and still show up again tomorrow.

Courage · Step Four
05 — Integrity Step Five

Integrity

Bringing your insides and outsides closer together.

When you speak your story out loud, shame loses its power. You become someone you can trust with the truth.

Integrity · Step Five
06 — Willingness Step Six

Willingness

Letting go of what’s killing you, one finger at a time.

You don’t have to be ready all at once. Start with: “I’m open to being changed here.”

Willingness · Step Six
07 — Humility Step Seven

Humility

Knowing your size—and that it’s enough.

Humility isn’t groveling. It’s standing at your actual height and asking for help you can’t manufacture alone.

Humility · Step Seven
08 — Love Step Eight

Love

Letting compassion lead the cleanup.

Love is not a feeling here. It’s a decision: I’ll become willing to repair the damage I caused.

Love · Step Eight
09 — Discipline Step Nine

Discipline

Making living amends, not just speeches.

Discipline is quiet: showing up, following through, staying out of harm’s way while you repair what can be repaired.

Discipline · Step Nine
10 — Perseverance Step Ten

Perseverance

Staying in the maintenance, not the meltdown.

You keep sweeping the floor. You keep watching your part. You don’t wait for the mess to drown you again.

Perseverance · Step Ten
11 — Awareness Step Eleven

Awareness

Listening for guidance instead of panic.

Prayer, meditation, quiet. You tune the station, turn down the volume of fear, and see what comes through.

Awareness · Step Eleven
12 — Service Step Twelve

Service

Giving away what you’ve been given.

You carry the message by living it: showing up sober, kind, honest, available. You become the proof that change is possible.

Service · Step Twelve
